Oregon Experience

Where to Start?

Oregon is home to a vast amount of mountains, valleys, gorges, wildlife, and other natural wonders. And they're accesible! Use this webpage to guide you on your next adventure through the great Oregon natural lands.

Dispersed camping is the practice of camping in the wild outside of recreational campground areas. It's a great way to escape the crowds and find your own place. Dispersed camping requires cleanliness, so remember: Pack it in, pack it out! Click the Forest Service logo for more information on dispersed camping.

Nice view!

High Rocks is an amazing viewpoint deep in the woods of the Mt. Hood National Forest. It took hours of driving through old Forest Service roads to find this beautiful view of Mt. Hood. Simply set up a tent at the edge of the cliff, crack a beer and enjoy the sunset! Click the pin for the location.

The road to badger lake is extremely rough. It's essential to make the trip in a vehicle with high clearance and either 4-wheel drive or all-wheel drive. Only attempt this trip if you care comfortable driving at very slow speeds over large rocks and small boulders.

Badger lake is brimming with beautiful mountain rainbow trout. These fish can be tricky to catch, but they're delicious once they're in the pan. I reccomend using a spinner if you're casting off the bank, but the real fun requires a canoe. Troll near the bottom of the lake for best results. These tasty fish are best when fried with lemon and butter.
